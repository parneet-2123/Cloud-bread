What is ZeroHunger

Goal 2: Zero Hunger

Goal 2 of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals is Zero Hunger. This goal aims to end hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture by 2030 1. In 2020, between 720 million and 811 million persons worldwide were suffering from hunger, roughly 161 million more than in 2019. Also in 2020, a staggering 2.4 billion people, or above 30 per cent of the world’s population, were moderately or severely food-insecure, lacking regular access to adequate food.

Problem Faced

Food waste is a major issue in our world today. Every year, billions of pounds of food are thrown away while millions of people go hungry. This is not only a moral issue but also an environmental one, as food waste contributes to greenhouse gas emissions and other negative impacts on our planet. At the same time, many restaurants struggle with excess food at the end of the day. This food is often still safe to eat but may not be able to be sold or served the next day. As a result, it often ends up in the trash. We saw this as an opportunity to make a difference. By connecting food banks and restaurants, we can help reduce food waste and provide meals to those in need. Our platform, Zero Hunger, was created to address this problem and help achieve the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 2: Zero Hunger.

ZEROHUNGER

Goal 2

Hunger is among the leading causes of death worldwide. Our earth has supplied us with plenty of resources, but unequal access and inadequate management have resulted in millions of people becoming hungry. Zero Hunger is an online platform that connects food banks with restaurants in order to donate excess food. Our objective is to completely eradicate food waste and make a contribution to the UN's Sustainable Development Goal 2: Zero Hunger. We collaborate with restaurants to donate food that is still edible, and if the food is no longer consumable, we decompose it to create manure. This manure is subsequently sold to farmers at cheaper prices, which also encourages sustainable agriculture. In addition, we gather vegetable peels and other restaurant food waste to manufacture colored paper. This not only reduces waste but also provides an eco-friendly alternative to traditional paper production. Click "DONATE NOW" to donate food.
